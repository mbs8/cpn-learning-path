# Marathon Preparation Nutrition Plan

## Daily Caloric Needs
- **BMR:** ~1,700 calories
- **With training:** 2,800–3,500+ calories/day (depending on mileage)
- **Adjust based on weekly running volume**

---

## Macronutrient Targets

| Nutrient | Daily Amount | Purpose |
|----------|---|---|
| **Carbohydrates** | 8–12g per kg body weight (576–864g) | Fuel for long runs; glycogen storage |
| **Protein** | 1.6–2.0g per kg (115–144g) | Recovery and muscle repair |
| **Fat** | 1.2–1.5g per kg (86–108g) | Energy and hormone support |

---

## Daily Eating Strategy

### **Breakfast** (Pre-training fuel)
- Oatmeal with banana, honey, and berries
- Whole grain toast with almond butter
- Coffee or tea

### **Mid-Morning Snack**
- Greek yogurt with granola
- Or: Energy bar + sports drink

### **Lunch** (Carb-focused)
- Chicken/fish with brown rice or pasta
- Vegetables (sweet potato, broccoli)
- Olive oil dressing

### **Afternoon Snack**
- Rice cakes with peanut butter
- Sports drink during/after training
- Dates or energy gel for long runs

### **Dinner**
- Lean protein (salmon, lean beef, tofu)
- Pasta or quinoa (2+ cups cooked)
- Salad with olive oil

### **Evening**
- Chocolate milk, banana, or smoothie

---

## Hydration Strategy

- **Daily:** 3–4 liters of water
- **During runs:** 500–800ml per hour (sports drink with 6–8% carbs)
- **Post-run:** 1.5L per kg body weight lost (within 4 hours)

---

## Carb-Loading Week (Before Race)

- Days 1–3: Taper running, maintain normal diet
- Days 4–7: Increase carbs to **10–12g/kg**, reduce fiber slightly
- Focus on: Pasta, rice, bread, potatoes, sports drinks

---

## Key Tips

✅ Eat 2–3 hours before long runs  
✅ Practice race-day nutrition during training  
✅ Prioritize sleep (7–9 hours) for recovery  
✅ Consider iron-rich foods (endurance demands)  
✅ Don't try anything new race week
